Tour To Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal,'Crown of the Palace', is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the
southern bank of the river Yamuna in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan (reigned
from 1628 to 1658) to house the tomb of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal; it also houses the tomb of Shah Jahan himslef. The tomb is
the centerpiece of a 17-hecter (42-acre) complex, which includes a mosque and a guest house, and is set in formal gardens bounded
on three sides by a crenellated

Construction of the mausoleum was essentially completed in 1643, but work continued on other phases of the projects for another 10
years. The Taj Mahal complex is believed to have been complete its entirety in 1653 at a cost estimated at the time to be around
32 million rupees, which in 2020 should be aproximately 70 billion rupees (about U.S. $916 million). The construction employed
some 20,000 artisans under a guidance of a board of architects led by the court architect to the emperor, Ustad Ahamad Lahauri.

The Taj Mahal was designed as UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for being "the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the
universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage". It is regarded by many as the best example of Mughal architecture and a
symbol of Indian rich history. The Taj Mahal attracts 7-8 million visitors a year and in 2007, it was declared as a winner of the new 7
Wonders of the World (2000-2007) initiative.

Christ the Redmeer (Portuguese: Cristo Redentor, Standred Brazilian Portuguese: Cristo Redentor, Local portuguese: Cristo Redentor)
is an Art Deco statue of the Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, created by French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by
Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, in collaboration with French engineer Albert Caquot. Romanian sculptor Gheorghe
Leonida fashioned the face. Constructed between 1922 and 1931, the statchue is 30 meters (98 ft) high, execluding its 8 meter (26 ft)
Peestal. The arm stretch is 28 meters (92 ft)wide.

The statue weighs 635 metric tons (625 long, 700 short tons), and is located at the perk of the 700-meter (2,300 ft)Corcovado
mountain in the Tijuca Forest National Park overlooking the city of Rio de Janeiro. A symbol of Christianity across the world, the
statue has also the cultural icon of both Rio de Janeiro and Brazil, and is listed as one of the New 7 Wonders of the World. It
is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone.

Machu Picchu is a 15th-centuary Inca citadel, located in the Eastern Cordillera of the Southern Peru, on the 2,430 meter (7,970 ft)
moutain rigde. It is located Machupicchu District within Urubamba Province above the scared valley, which is 80
kilometers (50 mi) northwest of Cuzco. The Urubamba River folws past it, cotting through the Cordillera and creating a canyon
with the tropical mountain climate

Most archaeologists believe that Machu Picchu was constructed as an estate for the Inca emperor Pachacuti (1438-1472). Ofen
mistakenly reffered to as "The Lost City of the Incas", it is the most familiar icon of te Inca civilization. The Incas built the estate
around 1450 but abandoned it a centuary later the time of the Spanish conquest although Known locally, it was not to know to
the Spanish during the colonial period and remained unknown to the outside world until Amarican Historian Hiram Bingham
brought it to international attention in 1911.

Machu Picchu was built in the classical Inca style, with polised dry-stone walls. Its three primary structures are the Intihuatana
the Temple of the Sun, and the Room of the Three Windows. Most of the outlying buildings have been reconstructed in order to
give tourests a better idea of how they originally appeared. By 1976, 30% of Machu Picchu has been restored and
and restoration continues.

Machu Picchu was decleared a Peruvian Historic Sanctuary in 1981 and a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983. In 2007,
Machu Picchu was voted one of the New Seven Wonders Of The World in worldwide internet poll.

Tour To Chinchen Itza

Chinchen Itza was a large preColombian city built by Maya people of the Terminal Classic period. The
archaeological site is located in Tinum Municipality, Yucatan state, Mexico

Chenchen Itza was a major focal point in the Northen Maya Lowlands from the Late classic (c.AD 600-900) through
the Terminal Classis (c.AD 800-900) and into the early portion of the Postclassic period (c.AD 900-1200). The site
exhibits a multitude of architectural styles, remeniscent of styles seen in central Mexico and of the Puuc and Chenes
styles of the Northen Maya lowlands. The presence of central Mexcian styles wad once thought to have been
representative of direct migration or even consequent from central Mexico, but most contemperory interpretations view
the presence of these non-Maya styles more as the result of cultural deffusion.

Chinchen Itza was one of the largest maya cities and it was likely to have been one of the mythical great cities, or
Tollans, referred to in later Mesoamerican literature. The city may have had the most diverse populatin in the Maya
world, The factor that could have contributed to the variety of architectural styles at the site.

The ruins of the Chenchen Itza are fedral property, and the site's stewardship is maintained by Mexcios Instituto Nacional
de Antropologia e Historia (National Institute of Antropology and History). The land under the monument had been
privately owned until 29 March 2010, when it was purchased by the state of Yucatan.
Chinchen Itza is one of the most visited archaeological sites in Mexcio with over 2.6 million tourests in 2017.

Tour to The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China(pinyin:Wanli Changcheng) is the collective
name of a series of fortifaction system generally built across the historical northern borders of China to protect and consolidate
territories of Chinese states and empires against various nomadic groups of the steppe and their polities. Several walls were being built
from as early as the 7th centuary BC by ancient Chinese states; selective stretches were later joined together by Qin Shi Huang (220-
206 BC), the first emporer of China. Little of the Qin wall remains. Later on, many successive dynasties have built and maintained
multiple stretches of border walls. The most well-known sections of the wall were built by the Ming dynasty (1368-1644).

Apart from defence, other purpose of the Great Wall have included border controls, allowing the imposition of duties of goods
transported along the Silk Road, regulation of encouragement of trade and the control of the immigration and emigration. Furthermore,
the defencive characterictics of The Great Wall were inhanced by the construction o watch towers, troop barracks, garrison stations,
singaling capabilities through the means of smoke or fire, and the fact that the path of the Great Wall also served as a transportation
corridor

The frontire wall built by different dynasties have multiple courses. Collectively, they strech from Liaodong in the east to Lop Lake in
the west, from the present-day Sino-Russian border in the north to Tao River(Taohe) in the south; along an arc that roughly delaneates
the edge of the Mongolian steppe. A comprehensive archaeological survey, using advanced techonologies, has concluded that the walls
built by the Ming dynasty measure 8,850 km (5,500 mi). This is made up of 6,259 km(3,889 mi) sections of actual wall, 359 km
(223 mi) of trenches and 2,232 km(1,387 mi) of natural defencive barriers such as hills and rivers. Another archaeological survey
found that the entire wall with all of its branches measures out to be 21,196 km(13.171 mi). Today, the defensive system of the Great
Wall is generally rechonised as one of the most impressive architectural feast in history.

Tour to Roman Colosseum

TheColosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheater (Latin:Amphitheatrum Flavium; Italian:
Anfiteatro Flavio or colosseo, is an oval amphitheatre in the center of the city of Rome, Italy. Built of
travertine limestone, tuff (volcanic rock), and brick-faced concrete, it was the largest amphitheatre ever built at the time and held
50,000 to 80,000 spectators. The Colosseum is just east of the Roman Forum. Constrution began under the eperor Vespasian in
AD 72 and was completed in AD 80 under his successor and hier, Titus. Further modifications were made during the reign of
Domitian (81-96). These three emperors known as the Flavian dynasty, and the amphitheatre was named in Latin for its
accioation with their family name(Flavius).

The Colosseum could hold an estimated 50,000 to 80,000 spectators at various points of its history over the centuaries, having an
average audiance of some 65,000; it was used for gladiatorial countests and public spectacles such as mock sea battles (for only a
short time as the hypogeum was soon filled in with mechnisms to support the other activities), animal hunts, executions, re-
enactments of famous battles, and dramas based on Roman Mythology. The Building ceased to be used for the entertainment in the early
medieval era. It was later reused for such purposes as housing, workshops, quarters for a relegious order, a fortress, a quarry, and a
Christian shrine.

Although substantially ruined because of the earthquakes and stone-robbers, the Colosseum is still an iconic symbol of Imperial Rome and
is listed as one of the New 7 Woners of The World. It is one of Rome's most popular tourests attractions and also has links to the
Roman Catholic Church, as each Good Friday the Peope leads a tourchilt "Way of the Cross" procession that starts in the aea around
the Coloseum
